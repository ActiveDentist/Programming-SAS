(Author: Roland Rashleigh-Berry                                            Date: 07 May 2007)

Introduction

If you decide to use Spectre and wish to enter into a support contract with me then you will have to agree to all the definitions, conditions and terms in this document before you proceed further. If one or more of these terms, conditions or definitions are not acceptable to your organization then it will not be permissible to enter into a support contract with me. I will require explicit agreement in the form of a signed copy of this page from a prospective client that all these terms, conditions and definitions are accepted by them before I will consider entering into a support contract.

Definitions

  • Spectre is defined as the clinical reporting system that exists on this web site. This means that copies of the system that exist on a client's site do not constitute "Spectre".
  • A support contract covers support for Spectre as defined above. It excludes the client's copy of Spectre by definition.

Disclaimer

  • If you use Spectre you do so at your own risk and accept that any loss or damage that results from using Spectre must be borne by the client and that the author of Spectre and the person providing support are not to be held responsible in whole or in part for any loss or damage that is the result.
  • None of the code in Spectre, as provided, has been validated for use by the client. Use of Spectre for reporting clinical data will be covered by industry regulations and the client's own Standard Operating Procedures and the client must comply with these.
  • Although claims are made on this web site about the speed and efficiency of Spectre, this is not guaranteed. Clients should instead regard these claims as a optimal estimate (in the author's opinion) of its capabilities that they should strive in the direction to achieving and that these benefits might never be realized.

The support service provided

  • The service provided is to maintain and enhance Spectre (as defined above) in a way that it can be used as a generic reporting tool for any who wish to use it. It should be clear from the previous sentence that support work goes into Spectre itself and the paying client is merely the indirect beneficiary as a user of Spectre.
  • I fix bugs for free. Apart from code changes and code enhancements to Spectre itself, the service provided will mostly take the form of enhancing the documentation of the Spectre system to hopefully assist the users of Spectre and to advise clients on how to make efficient use of the software. The documentation effort of this project stands at about 6:1 compared with all other activities so "documentation" is mostly what your money buys (about a third of the money is to cover the set-up costs of Spectre). This will be driven by requests from the users of Spectre.
  • Clients with support contracts of the types "Pharma Giant" and "Smaller Pharmas and global CROs" will have the existing CDISC reporting macros that I have written made available for their use while the support contract lasts. They will remain my copyrighted works and when the support contract ends or drops down to the "Pay as you go" type then the client is no longer allowed to use these CDISC reporting macros.

Conditions of the support service

  • Any code or documentation written as part of a support contract will be entirely owned by myself and may be provided to other users of Spectre as I see fit.
  • Requests for enhancements will be considered but not necessarily acted upon. Requests from clients with the highest level of support contract will be given priority. The decision on which enhancements will be implemented, how and when, lays with me as I have to ensure that Spectre remains a stable and generic product suitable for a wide range of users and not just the paying client or clients.
  • Bugs that are reported to me must be sent using minimal code and data that I can run myself against Spectre (as defined above). Data should be dummy data in a form such as is provided on this web site to demonstrate the %npcttab and %unistats macros. The code and data should demonstrate the supposed bug and nothing else. The exact nature of the perceived problem should be precisely stated. A reported bug is deemed to be a bug only if it manifests itself against the Spectre as defined above.
  • The decision on what constitutes a bug and the priority and time scales for fixing it rests entirely with myself.
  • Each client must appoint a Spectre "expert" and issues to do with Spectre software and documentation must go through this single point of contact. This single point of contact can be a single person or more than one person in a single support team. The client should inform me who this single point of contact is. The client must give at least a week's notice to inform me of a change to this. Only one single point of contact per organization is allowed per support contract. This also applies to global organizations.
  • The Spectre "expert" at the client's end should be fully familiar with Spectre documentation and should attempt to resolve issues to do with Spectre usage themselves. In this way they are not merely a routing point for sending me issues to do with Spectre but rather the end point and "expert" within their organization for Spectre issues. They should check any Spectre bug reported to them from within their organization themselves and only pass on bugs to me if they deem them to be genuine and a problem to do with Spectre (as defined above) and then only in the minimal form that is specified above.
  • The client's Spectre "expert" should maintain a FAQ within their organization and make it accessible to programmers within their organization who use Spectre. This does not imply that the "expert" should try not to communicate with me. They are free to communicate to me issues that they consider important about Spectre even if they were able to resolve these issues themselves. I welcome useful additions to the FAQ on this web site and I will ask for a copy of the client's FAQ from time to time to help me improve the Spectre documentation but the decision as to whether it should be given to me in part or in whole rests with the client.
  • The only support provided by myself is to the client's Spectre "expert" and will be to do with Spectre only. It does not cover other types of support such as SAS programming advice, shell scripting, Cygwin or help with, or advice on, study reporting.

Conclusion

It will not be possible to enter into a support contract with myself unless all the definitions, conditions and terms in this document are accepted. A signed copy of this page will be required by myself before I can enter into such a contract.
