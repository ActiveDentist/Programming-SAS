Terms for Contracted Spectre (Clinical) Support

(Author: Roland Rashleigh-Berry                                                            Date: 05 Jul 2007)

Introduction

The Spectre clinical reporting system will remain a system that is free to anyone to use. However, what you get for free is limited. The aim of the free system is to allow you to understand how a clinical reporting system works and no more. I feel it meets that aim very well. But work on Spectre will continue behind the scenes, especially with regard to the creation of CDISC-compliant reporting macros. Those macros will never be webbed with here and only clients with a support contract with me will be allowed to use them. This document sets out the terms for contracted Spectre (Clinical) support.

Amending the software

The full set of Spectre components is very unlikely to work for you perfectly as found on this web site. For example, if you are running on a Linux or Unix platform and wish to use the bookmarked PDF facilities then the script "getitles" will most likely need amending so that it can pick up titles from the first ten lines of your output reports correctly. Some of the scripts that generate the scripts required to run all the derived dataset builds and the reports assume something about your program naming convention which may not be applicable at your site. Your standard program headers might be different so that scripts like "mybadprognames" that find and fix program names that do not match the file name will not work. There is likely a number of things that will need changing and you could benefit from specialized help during this process. These are the changeable components of Spectre that need to be tailored for your site but you should not change any of the macros.

Bugs in the software

Spectre is complex enough for you to assume that there are bugs in the software. It depends on the way you use Spectre. You may use Spectre in a way that has already been thoroughly debugged such that you may never encounter a single bug even after years of use. Alternatively, you may use Spectre in an unexpected way that might show up several bugs almost immediately. You do not need a contract with me to have bugs fixed. I will fix them for free. If you do have a support contract with me then time fixing bugs will not be deducted for. What I ask of anyone who reports a bug is that they illustrate it with the simplest possible code that I can run for myself, preferably using just SAS® Learning Edition 4.1. If I am working on simultaneous bugs then I will give preference to those companies who are paying for the highest level of support, while at the same time bearing in mind the seriousness of the bug reported and its implications for other Spectre users.

General terms for contracted support

Any company entering into a support contract with me must provide a single point of contact through which requests are sent and responded to. This single point of contact might be a support team or a single person. This team or person should act as the expertise within the company for Spectre and requests should only be passed on to me if the issue can not be properly resolved by that single point of contact. Certainly, this point of support is expected to be fully acquainted with the latest version of Spectre documentation or refer to it if need be and to keep their own internal FAQ to ensure repeat questions are not passed on to me. This should in no way discourage this point of contact from passing on suggestions for improvements on to me or alerting me to problems encountered. I need to know such things. Also, if an internal FAQ is kept then I would like an up-to-date copy of that FAQ from time to time as I might like to add some of the questions to the FAQ kept on the web site. The only work covered by any support contract will be work on the reporting system itself or enhancements to it. The support contract does not cover general support for using SAS software, installing and using Cygwin, shell scripting or study reporting work. Any code written or documentation produced as part of the support contract will belong to me but may be freely used by the customer while they have a current support contract (except the "Pay as you go" type). This is one method I use to recover the set-up costs of this project. I might supply whatever documentation is written or code produced to other customers as I see fit.

Rates for contracted support

Contracted support will be based on the rate of 250 SFr (Swiss Francs) per hour (I live in Switzerland -- there is a currency converter here). This is higher than for regular programming work, since the work is done on an as-needed basis, but will be lower than most ad-hoc consultancy work. The figure includes recovering the set-up costs of this project which were roughly 160,000 SFr. If this reporting system becomes widely used then this rate of 250 SFr per hour is likely to reduce in the future, rather than  increase, perhaps down to as low as 200 SFr per hour (not inflation linked) if work on this reporting system becomes a full time job and the set-up costs have been recovered (any reduction in support rates will be announced on this page more than a year in advance). There are various levels of support as will be explained.

Pharma-Giant support

This is the highest level of support that is based on an estimated, but not limited to, four hours per week, fifty weeks per year coming to 50,000 SFr per year (not including sales tax). It is aimed at large pharmaceutical companies which will have 40+ programmers using the reporting system world-wide. These large pharmaceutical companies are the only ones who will be allowed to drive the reporting system forward. Spectre is a high production system that is written with large pharmaceutical companies in mind for their Phase II-IV studies. These large companies will likely have rationalized their reporting strategy and whatever approach they have decided upon will be of presumed benefit to other large pharmaceutical companies and will be a welcome addition to Spectre and will be given full consideration. The user guides, especially those for the two main reporting macros, will be heavily influenced by what these Pharma giants suggest. Four hours per week is not enough for extensive documentation upgrades but it is likely that there will be two or more pharmaceutical companies on this level of support and whatever is produced will be for their mutual benefit. Unlimited support will be given to developing reporting macros based on CDISC data specifications even if this far exceeds the four hours per week support estimate. This is the "set it up, explain how to use it*, keep it running and make it better" level of support and those companies will be given highest priority. Pharma giants are expected to keep this level of support throughout the life of the reporting system and they will have this time reserved for them should they wish to extend their contracts in future years.

Smaller Pharmas and global CRO support

This is the middle level of support that is based on an estimated two hours per week, fifty weeks per year coming to 25,000 SFr per year (not including sales tax). Is is aimed at middle sized to small pharmaceutical companies and global CROs with less that 40 programmers using the reporting system world wide. Requested enhancements to the reporting system will normally not be considered as Spectre will be driven by the Pharma giants instead but some consideration to these requests will be given. Suggestions as to how to improve the documentation and user guides will be welcomed. This is the "set it up, explain how to use it* and keep it running" level of support.

Pay as you go support

This is aimed at small pharmaceutical companies and small CROs using the free system (the webbed version) who already have the system set up and working but lack technical support and so need occasional help to keep the system running. Twenty-five hour blocks of help at 250 SFr per hour can be purchased in advance for 6250 SFr (not including sales tax). Time will be deducted from the total every time help is given. The minimum time deducted will be one half hour. You will not be charged for reporting genuine bugs and the time spent fixing them as bugs are fixed for free but note that bug fixes will in most cases be applied to the commercial system and not the free system. This is the "explain how to use it* and keep it running" level of support. This level of support excludes the use of any macros that are exclusively part of the commercial system.

CDISC support

I will be developing many macros to report on CDISC data. These will remain my copyrighted works but will be made available for use for those clients who have an active support contract of the "Pharma-Giant" and "Smaller Pharmas and global CRO" type. The client may make free use of these macros within their organisation while they have a support contract with me but if the support contract ends or drops down to the "Pay as you go" type then the clients are no longer allowed to use these macros.

Bug reporting

If you think you have spotted a bug then make sure you can reproduce this bug using minimal data and code. You will need to send both to me. By "minimal" is meant that what you send me should illustrate the bug and preferably nothing more. With this you should clearly state what the perceived bug is. If a value is incorrect then tell me exactly which value is incorrect and what it should be instead. Your data will have to be dummy data such as the data I created on my "Creating Test Datasets" page. You can always manipulate in your code the datasets I have created on the web site to illustrate the problem. I know this will use up your time but I have to be able to run this code on my own PC and recreate the bug. I am using SAS Learning Edition and I do not have procedures to read in SAS transport files. If I can not recreate the bug with what you send me or it is unclear to me what the bug is then I will assume that no bug exists. There are a million ways things could go wrong at the client's end but that is out of my control. The support I offer only covers the version of Spectre on this web site and any reported bug has to be recreatable at my end running against the macros I have webbed here. If the results are right for me but wrong for you then you will have to accept that in that circumstance there is no bug!! I have to make these rules so I don't spend all the hours of a day trying to track down bugs that do not exist. You must follow these rules if you send me a bug report or I will reject it and take no further actions to investigate it.

Conclusion

I would stress that a company is free to choose whomever they wish to give them support. Spectre is public domain software so support for it is not tied to any supplier and you are free to use Spectre without support. At the time of writing, the only supplier of support for Spectre that I recommend is myself. This may change in the future. Only support provided by me (or through me) will become part of Spectre in the future and be webbed here and made available to the public.
